### Phase 2: Hypothesis Development

#### Hypothesis Characteristics
- **Testable**: Can be proven/disproven with data
- **Specific**: Clear, not vague
- **Actionable**: Leads to recommendations
- **Prioritized**: Most likely/impactful first

#### Hypothesis Examples
- "Market share loss is driven by pricing, not product quality"
- "Customer churn is highest in the enterprise segment"
- "Operational inefficiency costs $50M annually"

### Phase 3: Data Collection & Analysis

#### Primary Research
- **Executive Interviews**: Strategic insights
- **Employee Surveys**: Organizational health
- **Customer Interviews**: Market insights
- **Shop Floor Observations**: Operational reality

#### Secondary Research
- **Market Sizing**: TAM, SAM, SOM
- **Competitive Benchmarking**: Best practices
- **Financial Analysis**: Performance drivers
- **Trend Analysis**: Industry dynamics

#### Analytical Techniques
| Technique | Use Case |
|-----------|----------|
| **Regression** | Identify drivers |
| **Segmentation** | Customer/business groups |
| **Benchmarking** | Performance comparison |
| **Scenario Planning** | Future possibilities |
| **Sensitivity Analysis** | Key variable impact |

### Phase 4: Synthesis & Insights

#### The "So What?"
- Don't just present data
- Tell the story
- Connect to business impact
- Make it memorable

#### Insight Development
```
Data â†’ Pattern â†’ Insight â†’ Implication

Example:
Data: Customer churn increased 20%
Pattern: Churn concentrated in SMB segment
Insight: SMBs feel underserved vs enterprise
Implication: Need dedicated SMB success team
```

### Phase 5: Recommendation Development

#### Recommendation Criteria
- **Specific**: Clear action items
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable impact
- **Achievable**: Realistic given constraints
- **Relevant**: Addresses core problem
- **Time-bound**: Clear timeline

#### The 80/20 Rule
- Focus on highest impact initiatives
- Don't boil the ocean
- Quick wins build momentum

## âš ï¸ Common Pitfalls

1. **Analysis Paralysis**: Over-researching, under-deciding
2. **Boiling the Ocean**: Trying to solve everything
3. **Cookie-Cutter Solutions**: Not tailoring to client
